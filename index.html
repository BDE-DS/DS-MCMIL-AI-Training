Curriculum Breakdown
Module by
Module.

Every module has a clear objective, specific topics, and a defined outcome. No filler. No theory without application.

MODULE 01
AI Literacy
Foundational Tool Agnostic
Objective
Build a shared understanding of what AI is, where it sits today, and why it is directly relevant to a steel manufacturing and export business.

Topics Covered
  • What AI actually is. How it moved from rule-based systems to language models that reason and execute.
  • The 2022 to 2025 shift. From AI giving advice to AI doing the work. What that means for MCMIL's Sales, Ops, and Finance teams specifically.
  • The automation reality. What percentage of tasks across Sales, HR, Procurement, and Finance are automatable right now, and what that number means for a 300-person team.
  • AI myths vs. reality. What it cannot do, where it hallucinates, and how to use it without blind trust.
  • Why this matters for a B2B manufacturer. Competitor analysis, export documentation, customer communication, procurement workflows.
Outcome

Every participant understands what AI is, why it is not optional anymore, and which of their own tasks are the first to go.

MODULE 02
Prompt Engineering
Tool Agnostic Hands-On
Objective
Teach participants how to communicate with any AI model so it produces finished, usable work instead of generic responses.

Topics Covered
  • Why most AI outputs fail. The difference between a vague instruction and a delegation.
  • The anatomy of a strong prompt. Role, context, task, format, and constraints. All five in every prompt.
  • Negative prompting. Telling AI what NOT to do is as important as what to do.
  • Prompt patterns for MCMIL use cases. Drafting export quotations, summarizing supplier conversations, writing customer-facing product descriptions for PPGI and GL products.
  • Iteration discipline. How to refine output in 3 passes without starting over every time.
Outcome

Every participant can write a prompt that produces a first draft worth editing, not one worth deleting.

MODULE 03
Generative AI
Tool Agnostic Governance
Objective
Give participants a working map of the GenAI landscape so they know what exists, what each category does, and where to apply it inside a manufacturing and export business.

Topics Covered
  • The GenAI landscape. Text, image, audio, video, and code generation. What each does and what it costs.
  • LLMs vs. agents vs. automation. What is the difference, and why the agent layer is where the real productivity is.
  • Real-world GenAI use cases from companies in steel, construction, and industrial supply that participants can map directly to their own roles at MCMIL.
  • Risk and governance. What not to feed into a public AI model. Data handling rules for a listed company like MCMIL.
  • Picking the right tool for the right task. The decision framework before you open any AI product.
Outcome

Participants can identify the right category of AI tool for any given task and explain the basic risk boundaries to their team.

MODULE 04
Claude Masterclass
Proprietary Live Demo Hands-On
Objective
Move participants from AI awareness to active delegation using Claude's full product suite, built around MCMIL's actual departments and daily workflows.

Topics Covered
  • Claude vs. everything else. Why it is built differently. No training on your data. Projects with memory. Agentic by design. The comparison that matters for a company handling sensitive B2B data and financial reports.
  • The 3 Claude products and when to use each. Claude Chat for thinking tasks, Claude Cowork for execution tasks that touch your actual files, Claude Code for technical builds and data processing at scale.
  • Building your memory system. Global instructions, claude.md files for project folders, and Skills. How MCMIL's Sales team sets up a folder for each key account. How Finance builds a monthly P&L skill that runs itself.
  • MCP connectors for MCMIL's stack. Connecting Claude to Google Drive, Gmail, Google Sheets, and Calendar. What changes when Claude can read your live data instead of a copy-paste.
  • Department use case walkthroughs across Sales, Finance, HR, Supply Chain, and Marketing. Participants see how each workflow is structured, what inputs Claude needs, and what the output looks like before they replicate it on their own.
Outcome

Every participant leaves with Claude Pro set up, one skill built, and a brain imprint plan tied to their specific function at MCMIL.
